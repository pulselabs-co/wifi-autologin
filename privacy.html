Privacy Policy — WiFi AutoLogin (PulseLabs Co)

Effective date: 2025-09-21

WiFi AutoLogin (published by PulseLabs Co) helps users automatically log into captive-portal Wi-Fi networks used by hostels, colleges, and similar networks. This page explains what data the extension uses and how it is handled.

What data the extension stores

If you check "Remember me" in the extension popup, your portal credentials (username and password) are stored locally in your browser's chrome.storage.local. These credentials are stored on your device only and are not transmitted to PulseLabs Co or any third party by the extension.

Permissions

The extension requests only the following permissions, required for its functionality:

  • Host permissions for the portal host (for example: http://172.16.2.1/*) — used to detect and interact with the captive portal page.
  • tabs and scripting — to open a background tab and inject the login data into the portal form.
  • storage — to store credentials locally when you choose to remember them.
  • alarms — to periodically check connectivity and retry login when needed.
  • notifications — to notify you of success or failure of auto-login attempts.

What the extension does NOT do

  • It does not send any credentials or other user data to remote servers. All credential storage and login actions happen locally in the browser on your device.
  • It does not collect analytics, telemetry, or personally identifiable information.

How to remove stored credentials

Open the extension popup and click Clear to remove saved credentials. You may also remove stored data via Chrome's Extensions → Storage UI or by uninstalling the extension.
